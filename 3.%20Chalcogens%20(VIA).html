3. Chalcogens (VIA)

8th July 2020 at 4:57pm
2.4 The Chemistry of Groups

The chalcogens (Group VIA or Group 16) are an eclectic group of nonmetals and metalloids. While not as reactive as the halogens, they are crucial for normal biological functions.

They each have six electrons in their valence electron shell and, due to their proximity to the metalloids, generally have small atomic radii and large ionic radii. They will typically take on an oxidation state of +2 or +6.

Oxygen is the most important element in this group for many reasons; it is one of the primary constituents of water, carbohydrates, and other biological molecules.

Sulfur is also an important component of certain amino acids, and vitamins.

Selenium also is an important nutrient for microorganisms and has a role in protection from oxidative stress.

The remainder of this group is primarily metallic and generally toxic to living organisms. It is important to note that, at high concentrations, many of these elements—no matter how biologically useful—can be toxic or damaging.
