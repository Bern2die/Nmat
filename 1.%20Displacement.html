1.3 Displacement and Velocity

An object in motion may experience a change in its position in space, known as displacement (x or d).

This is a vector quantity and, as such, has both magnitude and direction.

The displacement vector connects (in a straight line) the object’s initial position and its final position - representing the net change in position from initial to final.

Distance (d) traveled, on the other hand, considers the pathway taken and is a scalar quantity.
