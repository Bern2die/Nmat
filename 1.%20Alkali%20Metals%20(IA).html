2.4 The Chemistry of Groups

The alkali metals (Group IA or Group 1) possess most of the classic physical properties of metals, except that their densities are lower than those of other metals.

The alkali metals have only one loosely bound electron in their outermost shells. They will typically have an oxidation state of +1.

Their Z,,eff,, values are very low, giving them the largest atomic radii of all the elements in their respective periods.

This low Z value also explains the other trends:

  • Low ionization energies
  • Low electron affinities
  • Low electronegativies

Alkali metals easily lose one electron to form univalent cations, and they react readily with nonmetals—especially the halogens—as in NaCl
